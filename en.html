Background information

I began my career in the 3D sector in 2015, transitioning into technical 3D development over the last five years. Throughout my journey, I’ve acted as a Swiss Army knife, combining 3D artistry with programming expertise to design systems and drive productions across diverse fields, including architectural visualisation, product design, AR/VR development, and software engineering.

Computer graphics have always fascinated me, offering the ability to reconstruct reality and then transform it into something entirely new. For me, this field represents the convergence of art and science—a space where the impossible becomes possible, and the invisible becomes visible. It’s the ultimate medium for storytelling and revealing complex processes through visual and interactive means.

My first foray into computer graphics was through a passion project, Ghent Theft Auto, where I ambitiously set out to recreate the entire city of Ghent within a video game. This self-driven endeavor laid the groundwork for my later technical and artistic pursuits.

During my studies, I co-founded Gentcement, an architecture blog that explored urban planning and construction in Ghent, alongside architect Fréderic Louis. I also interned at Fisheye, gaining experience in creative production. In parallel, I worked temporary jobs, including constructing decorations for Tomorrowland’s camping grounds, both in workshops and on-site.

My first 3D artist full-time job was at the architectural visualisation firm Nanopixel. While living and working in Roeselare for just over a year, I was introduced to working in a visual production team and the workflows along the various stages of the visualisation process.

During my time at Nanopixel, I founded a VJ duo together with a good friend of mine, under the name of LUMEN. In our first year of operations, LUMEN proudly worked on over ten editions for five different customers in four different venues, including the Concertzaal in Vooruit Kunstencentrum and the Kontrolekamer at Kompass Klub.

After my first job, I started moving towards a more technical role at POW 3D Design in Zele. Here I applied my skills towards optimising the company's production workflow, providing conceptual and technical support when chasing leads, while also making product animations, interactive visuals, 360° video applications. My proudest achievement was a real-time interactive VR experience where users could explore the fully automated production line of Europe's biggest dough factory.

From 2018 onwards, I really got into programming in 3D environments. When I started working as a 3D Engineer at In The Pocket on a dedicated Augmented Reality team, a new world really opened up for me. Working in a a versatile company with cross-functional teams allowed me to solidify my knowledge of real-time applications like augmented reality and broader domains such as software architecture and mobile app development. While working at In The Pocket, I was able explore entirely new domains such as agile development and C# programming and make them my own. I was able to design the artwork for Shift 2018, developed dynamic navmesh generation solutions in Unity, worked on mobile geodata applications.

In 2021 I returned to high-end visualisation as a Technical Lead at Animotions and Visuals for Brands. I applied my software engineering expertise to architectural and product visualisation, bridging the gap between creative and technical teams. I upgraded and maintained the on-premise render farm and automated various aspects of the day-to-day operations using Python. I also used Python extensively to manage distributed rendering workflows and asset pipelines using Blender and 3DS Max, while also utilising the pytest framework to run validation tests for 5000+ 3D assets.

My proudest achievements are developing a real-time product visualizer for Royal Botania using React and Three.js and co-authoring a €200K VLAIO subsidy dossier focused on modernizing visualisation pipelines for Flemish SMEs through the use of 3D and AI.
